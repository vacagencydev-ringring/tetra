## Strike comes from many sources

Big CP gains come from stacking many medium sources, not one huge upgrade. Missing low-cost sources (pet understanding, titles, collection bonuses) hurts more than under-investing in one expensive item.

**Fill missing categories first**, then push premium upgrades (soul imprint, high-tier refinement).

---

## Daevanion (Markutan) board

Board progression gives offense and defense:
- Damage amplification / resistance
- Penetration / mitigation
- Hit, crit, defense, block, anti-crit
- Fixed and % HP scaling

Progression compresses multiple stat dimensions. It improves actual combat consistency, not just sheet CP.

---

## Medals and equipment

**Medal routes:** Season missions, weekly missions, Abyss rewards, exchange shops, material conversion.

Secure a stable weekly medal floor first. Add variable sources (e.g. transformation events) after the baseline is set.

---

## Ludra vs crafted gear

When comparing equipment, check:
- Slot and option structure
- Max potential lines
- Re-upgrade cost after replacement
- Use case (PVE boss, PVP, mixed)

Item level alone is misleading. A “sidegrade” can waste kinah if you ignore conversion and re-enhancement cost.

---

## Upgrade order

1. Complete missing low-cost strike sources (pet, titles, collection).
2. Unlock high-impact Daevanion nodes.
3. Lock in weekly medal routine.
4. Evaluate gear replacement with full conversion cost.
5. After each major gear change, re-check skill and role setup.

---

## Common mistakes

- **Overfocusing one slot:** Spread investment across missing systems first.
- **Ignoring post-replacement cost:** Include full recraft/reinforce budget.
- **Chasing sheet CP only:** Test in real dungeon or PvP.
- **Inconsistent medal collection:** Put medal tasks on a fixed weekly schedule.
