## Guide scope
- This guide targets practical progression players, not only top-end whales.
- It focuses on efficient upgrade order and cost-effective transitions.
- Core objective: avoid expensive side-grades while maintaining strong PVE output.

---

## Equipment progression path

Recommended route:
- Early crafted path -> higher crafted dragon path.
- Dungeon guarder -> crafted guarder or Ludra guarder transition.

Important principle:
- If weapon/guarder is still dungeon-tier, stop side spending and push core crafted upgrades first.
- Core weapon and guarder determine most of your practical damage curve.

---

## Weapon and guarder priorities

Desired option profile:
- Power
- Accuracy
- Multi-hit related efficiency
- Attack speed / cast-speed relevant value
- Boss damage related value

General advice:
- Treat weapon and guarder as your top two investment pillars.
- Upgrade these before expensive cosmetic or low-impact side systems.

---

## Accessory framework

Earrings/Necklace:
- Prioritize attack, critical, and accuracy baselines.
- Fill remaining slots with utility/offense choices by budget.

Rings:
- Ring setup changes with arcana plan and active-skill slot strategy.
- Keep your ring plan aligned with your final rotation concept.

Bracelets:
- Time / Destruction / Wisdom style combinations are commonly used.

---

## Armor and stat slot logic

- Armor should support consistency first, then damage scaling.
- Use slot decisions that stabilize hit/crit first before greed options.
- Avoid overinvesting in temporary armor pieces if major upgrade is near.

---

## Socket and stone direction

Gem direction:
- Aim for stable critical + hit baseline across all pieces.
- Build consistency before pushing luxury max-roll goals.

Godstone direction:
- One movement-impact control option is commonly useful.
- Remaining slots can be tuned to your farm environment and team setup.

---

## Upgrade traps to avoid

- Side-grading too many times between similar tiers.
- Spending heavily on pieces that will be replaced soon.
- Ignoring opportunity cost of delayed weapon/guarder completion.

---

## Weekly practical checklist

- [ ] Is my current weapon tier blocking progression speed?
- [ ] Is guarder investment synchronized with weapon level?
- [ ] Am I spending on side-grades instead of major upgrades?
- [ ] Did I validate hit/crit stability in actual dungeons?
- [ ] Are ring/arcana choices aligned with my real rotation?

---

## Short build philosophy
- Strong Ranger progression is less about one perfect piece and more about disciplined order.
- Prioritize core damage foundation, then optimize detail layers.

---

## Practical conclusion
For Ranger PVE, efficient progression order wins over flashy short-term upgrades. Commit first to weapon/guarder power spikes, maintain stable hit/crit floor, and only then invest in high-cost optimization layers.
