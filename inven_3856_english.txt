## Guide scope
- This is a high-efficiency PVE route, focused on upper-end performance.
- It is written as an update over older baseline Assassin guides.
- Main theme: Phantom Clone cooldown reduction flow and practical rotation control.

---

## Build identity

Primary identity:
- Cooldown-centric loop with Phantom Clone synergy.
- High value from clean sequencing rather than pure static stat stacking.

What changed from older versions:
- Skill-speed baseline and patch changes altered investment breakpoints.
- The guide compares practical outcomes of Lv16 vs Lv20 decisions.

---

## Skill investment logic

## Key question: Ambush Lv16 or Infiltration Lv20?
- Ambush Lv16 can already provide strong practical uptime in many encounters.
- Infiltration Lv20 can outperform in longer windows and specific pattern sets.
- Final decision should follow your dungeon role, manual control comfort, and team pace.

## Priority concept
- Do not upgrade randomly to Lv20.
- Upgrade in the order that improves your real rotation uptime first.
- Test each breakpoint inside actual boss patterns, not only training scenarios.

---

## Stigma and shard scaling

- Stigma setup changes with shard availability.
- Low-shard players should use stability-first routing.
- High-shard players can push more aggressive cooldown reset routes.
- The guide includes a practical split by progression stage.

---

## Rotation and slot setup philosophy

Slot design in this guide:
- Trigger priority when key windows are active.
- Separate fast slash and tiger-style utility usage for manual control.
- Purpose: reduce Ambush cooldown manually under pattern pressure.

Important note:
- Macro-only play can become inefficient when Phantom Clone state is active.
- Manual adjustment during clone windows usually gives better real DPS.

---

## Cooldown timing interpretation

- Ambush crit-damage amplification window is short but highly valuable.
- With enough cooldown reduction, real combat cycle can approach very low downtime.
- Correct weaving matters more than theoretical one-button sequence.

---

## Practical dungeon execution

## In burst windows
- Pre-align cooldown reducers.
- Enter with clean opener and avoid overstacking delayed buttons.
- Keep clone-linked windows for high-impact moments.

## In pattern-heavy fights
- Preserve movement-safe buttons.
- Delay low-value animation lock skills if boss movement is imminent.
- Prioritize uptime and consistency over risky greed casts.

---

## Common mistakes
- Overcommitting to macro during clone windows.
- Upgrading all Lv20 targets before testing real fight value.
- Forcing one static sequence in all bosses.
- Ignoring party speed when choosing personal investment path.

---

## Recommended workflow
1. Choose baseline cooldown route.
2. Validate Ambush/Infiltration breakpoint in real boss runs.
3. Adjust stigma by shard progression.
4. Refine slot order for your hand comfort and latency.
5. Re-test after major patch notes.

---

## Practical conclusion
Assassin PVE performance is determined by execution quality and cooldown architecture. This guide's value is in building a controllable loop that stays strong across real boss mechanics, not just in ideal dummy conditions.
