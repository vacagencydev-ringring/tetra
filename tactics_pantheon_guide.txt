## Why AP matters for everyone
- Abyss Points are not only for aggressive PVP players.
- AP supports major progression systems, including accessories and skill-related advancement paths.
- Ignoring AP slows long-term account growth even in mostly PVE playstyles.

---

## Weekly AP acquisition architecture

Main acquisition families:
1) Emergency/weekly supply delivery
2) Shugo Festa rewards
3) Journal mission loops
4) Field order missions
5) Season weekly missions
6) Abyss order missions
7) Abyss corridor windows

Design principle:
- Mix stable sources with variable sources.
- Build your plan on stable floors first.

---

## Low-risk AP strategy for non-PVP players

Step 1:
- Lock in all low-conflict weekly AP routes.

Step 2:
- Use reroll/selection logic on mission boards to avoid high-friction tasks.

Step 3:
- Reserve danger-content AP only when entry conditions are favorable.

Step 4:
- Convert AP into progression in priority order; avoid random spending.

---

## Corridor timing value

- Corridor windows can offer high AP density when available.
- Treat corridor access as an event-based burst opportunity, not a guaranteed baseline.
- If corridor is open, prioritize it over lower-yield fallback loops.

---

## Mission board efficiency

- Not all mission cards are equal for AP per minute.
- A lower-reward but quick and safe mission can outperform risky objectives over a full week.
- Optimize for completion reliability, not single-run headline value.

---

## Common AP planning errors

Mistake: Overcommitting to risky tasks early in week.
Fix: finish stable AP floor first.

Mistake: Spending AP without progression priority.
Fix: predefine AP spend sequence before weekly loop starts.

Mistake: Ignoring refresh/reroll options.
Fix: actively reroll high-friction missions.

Mistake: Treating corridor as guaranteed.
Fix: keep corridor AP as bonus variable in planning.

---

## Practical weekly AP board

Monday-Tuesday:
- Secure low-friction AP routes.

Wednesday-Thursday:
- Midweek mission optimization and rerolls.

Friday-Sunday:
- Consume remaining high-value opportunities, including corridor if open.

This spread lowers reset-day panic and missed rewards.

---

## Checklist
- [ ] Stable AP floor sources are completed every week.
- [ ] Mission reroll logic is used to reduce time loss.
- [ ] AP spend order is pre-planned.
- [ ] Corridor access is tracked and exploited when open.
- [ ] High-risk AP routes are optional, not mandatory bottlenecks.

---

## Practical conclusion
Pantheon/Abyss growth is strongest when AP is managed as a weekly system, not random pickups. Build stable AP floors, add variable spikes, and spend AP with strict priority to sustain long-term account acceleration.
