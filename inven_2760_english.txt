## Guide scope
- This guide reflects practical Spiritmaster PVE execution with ongoing patch adjustments.
- It is not a static one-time build; recommendations are tied to recent behavior changes.
- Main focus: summon choice, specialization mapping, and stable real-fight damage flow.

---

## Core specialization map

Commonly used specialization pairs/sets include:
- Cold Shock
- Flame Burn
- Elemental Fusion
- Cooperative Curse
- Space Control
- Soul Scream
- Rapid Shot
- Shock Release

Summon-side specialization is also part of the rotation architecture:
- Fire spirit route
- Water spirit route
- Wind spirit route
- Earth spirit route

---

## Key breakpoint insight

- Recent motion and behavior changes increased the value of high-level fusion/co-op curse paths.
- Practical recommendation from the guide context: secure key high-level breakpoints where your real damage distribution shifts.
- This is especially relevant when curse-coop contribution becomes a larger slice of total output.

---

## Summon selection in real PVE

Current practical trend:
- Water and fire summons are the most frequently used in common PVE scenarios.

Why this matters:
- The summon choice changes both your skill rhythm and your practical utility profile.
- Build your specialization and cooldown order around your primary summon, not the other way around.

---

## Passive investment perspective

- Not every passive gives equal practical value in current PVE flow.
- Some passives may appear strong on paper but underperform in real boss uptime.
- Prioritize passives that reinforce your active rotation consistency and summon uptime.

---

## Rotation principles

1. Open with stable setup rather than immediate greed burst.
2. Maintain curse/fusion pressure through reliable windows.
3. Re-anchor around summon-friendly cycles after movement mechanics.
4. Avoid overcommitting to long-lock skills during uncertain boss movement.

---

## Practical execution notes

- Track boss movement and phase changes before spending major cooldowns.
- Preserve at least one mobility-safe sequence for recovery windows.
- Keep your summon state and your active skill rhythm synchronized.
- Rebuild quickly after forced interruptions instead of forcing old sequence timing.

---

## Common mistakes

- Treating Spiritmaster as a fixed one-loop class regardless of boss pattern.
- Investing heavily in low-impact passives while core cycle is unstable.
- Delaying summon breakpoint levels that unlock major practical value.
- Chasing theoretical peak over stable real uptime.

---

## Build maintenance checklist

- [ ] Is my main summon choice aligned with current content?
- [ ] Did I secure key fusion/co-op breakpoint levels?
- [ ] Are my passives supporting active cycle reliability?
- [ ] Can I keep pressure after movement-heavy mechanics?
- [ ] Did I retune after recent patches?

---

## Practical conclusion
Spiritmaster PVE performance scales through synchronization: summon selection, specialization breakpoints, and adaptable rotation flow. Stable execution across real mechanics beats rigid theoretical loops.
