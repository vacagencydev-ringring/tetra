## Scope
- Focuses on practical handling of new transcendence and tier 4-6 pattern pressure.
- Written for parties that want consistent clears, not one-time lucky clears.
- Main objective: reduce wipes through role clarity and mechanic discipline.

---

## First principle: clear mechanics before chasing logs

- Most wipe chains begin with one ignored mechanic.
- A short damage cut is usually faster than a full reset.
- Controlled phase progression beats random burst attempts.

---

## New transcendence dungeon pre-run setup

Before entering:
- confirm role assignment
- verify defensive skill timeline
- check required combat power and condition gates
- define revive and reset policy

During early attempts:
- map telegraph signals
- call movement clearly
- prioritize team survival over damage greed

After each wipe:
- identify exact failure trigger
- change one variable at a time

---

## Tier 4-6 systemic changes to respect

1) Additional transcendence traits increase complexity.
2) Kill requirements per area can be stricter.
3) Patterns are upgraded versions plus new cooperative mechanics.

Practical result:
- old low-tier habits can fail hard at higher tiers.

---

## Deus Research Base notes

- Soul knot mechanics can punish movement mistakes with slow + sustained damage.
- Final boss chain patterns require accurate pattern distinction.
- Some dialogue-triggered moments invert expected positioning behavior.

Team rule:
- use dialogue-linked call keywords to avoid chain confusion.

---

## Fragmented Arcanis notes

- Additional traps and floor pressure can stack avoidable damage quickly.
- Mid and final boss include cooperation-heavy mechanics.
- Failure often causes both survival loss and major DPS downtime.

Team rule:
- pre-assign mechanic roles: lure, release, call, backup.

---

## Communication protocol

Keep calls short and repeatable:
- "Hold burst"
- "Move out"
- "Stack in"
- "Break now"
- "Cleanse ready"

Avoid long explanations in active pattern windows.

---

## Recovery discipline

- Not every pull is recoverable efficiently.
- If major cooldown sync is broken, reset early.
- If pattern caller is uncertain, pause damage and re-stabilize.

This improves total clear speed over a full session.

---

## Weekly progression model

Week 1:
- pattern recognition and call vocabulary standardization

Week 2:
- movement cleanup and defensive timing refinement

Week 3:
- burst optimization only in verified safe windows

Skipping this sequence usually creates unstable runs.

---

## Team checklist

- [ ] Pattern caller and backup caller are assigned.
- [ ] Burst hold windows are known by all members.
- [ ] Mechanic responsibilities are pre-assigned.
- [ ] Reset rules are agreed before the run.
- [ ] Post-wipe one-line review is done every attempt.

---

## Practical conclusion
High-tier dungeon success is mostly operational execution. Parties that standardize calls, roles, and reset decisions clear faster and more reliably than parties that only rely on raw combat power.
