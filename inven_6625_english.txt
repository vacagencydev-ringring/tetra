## Guide scope
- This guide focuses on practical PVE skill priorities.
- It emphasizes cooldown flow, survivability, and stable uptime.
- The author notes that this is a recommendation, not a single mandatory build.

---

## Core active skills and specialization sets

## 1) Fierce Strike (Lv20 recommended)
- Recommended specializations: 3, 4, 5.
- Effects include multihit increase and Beho Strike cooldown reduction.
- At high investment, combo extension improves sustained output.

## 2) Continuous Flurry (Lv20 recommended)
- Two common routes:
- Route A: 2, 3, 4 for stronger Judgment frequency.
- Route B: 2(or3), 4, 5 for stronger Punishment cooldown flow.
- Choice depends on party uptime vs burst window preference.

## 3) Judgment (top priority to Lv20)
- Core specializations: 3, 4, 5.
- Key values: additional damage, guaranteed critical hit, recast removal.
- If survivability is weak, temporary life-steal option can be used.

## 4) Punishment split usage
- Utility mode (Lv12+): used primarily as a buff trigger.
- Damage mode (Lv20): used as a real DPS skill with anti-block/anti-evade value.
- This is one of the major identity forks in Gladiator PVE.

## 5) Optional heavy skill: Annihilation
- Strong damage profile but can feel less smooth for some players.
- Recommended when your execution and boss pattern knowledge are stable.

## 6) Debuff and mitigation helpers
- Weakening Blow (armor reduction utility) remains useful.
- Beho Strike gives defense utility and can provide party-level protection.

---

## Lv20 upgrade order (recommended)
1. Judgment
2. Fierce Strike (left-click core)
3. Continuous Flurry (right-click core)
4. Punishment mode completion
5. Optional damage utility skills

---

## Passive setup
- Frenzy (high value)
- Iron Wall Defense
- Blessing of Judgment
- Shock Accuracy
- Pain Block
- Vitality reinforcement

This passive package is designed to keep both consistency and tank-side comfort.

---

## Stigma recommendations
- Taunt (Lv15~20) as a key utility layer.
- Protection Shield (Lv5~20).
- Divine Punishment (Lv10~15).
- Flex slot: Ally Protection / Nezakan / Double Armor / Noble Armor.

Choose the final slot by content role:
- More mitigation when learning content.
- More damage support when farm patterns are stable.

## Arcana direction (example framework)
- Cup: vitality/time line.
- Compass: vitality/freedom line.
- Scroll: magic or vitality by survival needs.
- Bell: destruction line.
- Mirror: wisdom line.

Practical note:
- Scroll can be swapped based on whether your current bottleneck is HP or mitigation.

---

## Input and cancel execution

Common cancel patterns:
- Left -> Judgment -> Left -> Judgment.
- Skill queue OFF with held left-click and repeated right-click weaving.
- During Judgment-ready windows, faster repetitive input can be effective at high combat speed.

Hotkey concept:
- Right-click lane often includes Punishment, Judgment, and Continuous Flurry.
- Keep panic survival and utility buttons in reachable positions.

---

## Party role interpretation
- Gladiator can operate as a hybrid of frontline pressure and pattern control.
- Build decisions should match your party composition:
- More defensive route in low-heal parties.
- More aggressive cooldown route in optimized farm parties.

---

## Practical conclusion
The strongest Gladiator PVE setup is not just max level skills, but a clean Judgment-centered loop, correct Punishment mode choice, and stable input execution under real boss patterns.
