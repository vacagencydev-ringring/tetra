## Why this matters
- Most players lose kinah not because of low playtime, but poor scheduling.
- Multi-character farming can be strong, but only with controlled resource planning.
- This guide converts broad "farm more" advice into an actual weekly framework.

---

## 6-character weekly farming concept

Reported field example in the linked discussion:
- Approximate weekly kinah output in the 100M+ range under heavy routine execution.
- Higher output possible when all side drops are liquidated efficiently.

Important context:
- This is not universal guaranteed income.
- Real output varies by class efficiency, gear level, queue luck, and market timing.

---

## Energy-based run planning

Core formula mindset:
- Per-character weekly energy = passive recharge + shop support + crafting support.
- Convert total energy into practical run count by your content cost per run.
- Multiply by active roster size.
- Apply efficiency decay and fail-time buffer.

Never skip this planning step:
- If you skip the math, your week collapses near reset.

---

## Time-slot optimization for working players

Practical split:
- Mobile-friendly blocks: short tasks, checks, purchases, mission setup.
- Prime PC blocks: party-heavy content and high-value clears.
- Low-traffic blocks: solo content and cleanup tasks.

Weekly shape:
- Midweek: push core high-yield content while party supply is healthy.
- Weekend: consume ticket-type backlog and finish delayed tasks.

---

## Character role splitting

Do not run all alts identically.

Suggested role model:
- Energy burners (high-value consumption)
- Routine handlers (daily mission completion)
- Utility alts (specific resource loops)
- Optional AP/abyss support alts

Role splitting improves throughput and prevents bottlenecks.

---

## Liquidation strategy

- Resource value is not fixed over time.
- Selling everything immediately is not always optimal.
- Keep a simple sell policy:
- Immediate sell for high-volume low-volatility items.
- Timed sell for market-sensitive drops.

---

## Common mistakes

Mistake: Attempting full clear routine every day.
Fix: Assign content by weekday purpose.

Mistake: Ignoring reset windows.
Fix: Maintain visible weekly checklist with deadlines.

Mistake: Over-investing alt characters.
Fix: Keep alt spending below main-character progression threshold.

Mistake: No fallback plan for bad queue hours.
Fix: Prepare solo-compatible backup tasks per session.

---

## Operational checklist

- [ ] Weekly energy budget calculated per character.
- [ ] Prime-time and off-time tasks are separated.
- [ ] Alt roles are defined and stable.
- [ ] Reset deadlines are tracked.
- [ ] Sell policy exists for major drop categories.

---

## Practical conclusion
Kinah farming at scale is a scheduling problem first, combat problem second. If you control energy math, session timing, and alt roles, your weekly output rises while burnout drops.
