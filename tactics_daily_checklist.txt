## Goal
- Build a routine you can repeat every week without burnout.
- Reduce missed rewards caused by last-day panic.
- Convert random play into consistent progression.

---

## Routine design principles

Daily logic:
- prioritize tasks that lose value if skipped.

Weekly logic:
- spread high-load tasks across multiple days.

Resource logic:
- track consumables and weekly-limited resources numerically.

Consistency beats intensity.

---

## Recommended daily order

1) Check journal/mission board for high-value tasks.
2) Review energy and ticket leakage risk.
3) Complete short mobile-friendly blocks first.
4) Move party-dependent tasks into prime-time windows.

This sequence reduces decision fatigue and protects progress.

---

## Recommended weekly structure

Early week:
- lock core weekly rewards.

Midweek:
- run party-heavy or high-risk content.

Weekend:
- clean up remaining tickets, missions, and resource consumption.

Avoid putting everything near weekly reset.

---

## Odd Energy control

Core idea:
- Odd Energy should be budgeted weekly, not spent impulsively.

Management:
- estimate weekly inflow
- define target consumption windows
- avoid one-day over-spend

Random gains are bonus, not planning baseline.

---

## Time allocation by session length

20-30 min:
- mission checks + short guaranteed rewards

45-60 min:
- daily core loop + one progression side task

90+ min:
- include party content or backlog cleanup

Pick the block that matches real life schedule.

---

## Anti-chaos checklist

- [ ] daily must-do tasks are completed first
- [ ] party content is scheduled in active hours
- [ ] weekly-limited tasks are distributed across days
- [ ] odd/ticket consumption is monitored
- [ ] sunday pre-reset audit is performed

---

## Weekly board template

For each day:
- 3 daily fixed tasks
- 2 weekly accumulation tasks
- resource spend record

Sunday review:
- unresolved weekly rewards
- next week priority list

Simple templates outperform complex plans you never follow.

---

## Common failures and fixes

Failure: doing easy tasks only.
Fix: lock at least one high-impact task each session.

Failure: ignoring resource logs.
Fix: update a minimal numeric tracker daily.

Failure: postponing hard content to reset day.
Fix: move one hard block to midweek.

Failure: no routine adaptation.
Fix: review and adjust weekly after reset.

---

## Practical conclusion
The best daily/weekly checklist is the one you can execute repeatedly. A stable rhythm with tracked resources and scheduled difficulty gives better long-term growth than irregular high-effort sessions.
