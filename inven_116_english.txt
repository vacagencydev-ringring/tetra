## Guide scope
- Built for endgame Chanter players handling boss content.
- Includes hybrid role perspective: damage, healing, buffs, debuffs.
- Extended for higher-tier Nightmare context.

---

## Active skill priority structure

## Top tier
- Crushing core attack to high level.
- Main strike skills with movement/penetration value.

## Secondary tier
- White-heat strike line.
- Gap-close and rotational fillers.
- Rotation stabilizers with cooldown/value tradeoff.

## Utility tier
- Recovery and defensive support tools.
- Conditional trigger skills for specific boss moments.

The key is not maxing everything first, but building a rotation backbone that remains stable under pattern pressure.

---

## Rotation framework

Typical opener:
1. Team speed/cooldown support buff.
2. Spin/impact setup.
3. Core cancel sequence around left-click main strike.
4. Conditional burst fillers if trigger conditions are active.

Core execution pattern:
- Rotation Strike -> Wave Strike -> animation cancel chain -> conditional burst -> re-entry skill.
- Keep movement-capable skills available for mechanics.

---

## Animation cancel fundamentals

- Disable queued-skill dependency when practicing manual control.
- Use core left-click strike as cancel anchor.
- Weave right-click or short-cast skills at the right frame.
- Objective: preserve DPS uptime without getting locked in unsafe animations.

---

## Boss fight positioning

- Baseline assumption: stay close to boss for optimal loop.
- When forced out:
- Use ranged/long-reach fallback skills.
- Re-enter with dash, utility displacement, or short-gap skills.
- Resume normal rotation immediately after re-entry.

---

## Hybrid role in party composition

- Chanter performs best as a high-uptime hybrid.
- Stable setup usually includes at least one dedicated tank/healer partner.
- In undergeared parties, shift more into support uptime.
- In farm parties, shift more into aggressive cooldown routing.

---

## Skill specialization philosophy

- Prioritize effects that increase practical uptime and hit reliability.
- Movement conversion and cooldown reduction can outperform raw paper damage in real fights.
- Build for mechanic-heavy consistency first, then greed.

---

## Frequent mistakes

- Overusing long animation skills during unsafe phases.
- Ignoring support windows while chasing personal parse.
- Forcing one fixed sequence in every boss.
- Delaying defensive utility until too late.

---

## Practical weekly tuning checklist

- [ ] Is my cancel timing stable under latency?
- [ ] Are my movement skills reserved for real mechanics?
- [ ] Is support buff timing aligned with party burst windows?
- [ ] Did I adapt specialization for current dungeon tier?
- [ ] Can I maintain rotation after forced displacement?

---

## Practical conclusion
Chanter PVE excels when rotation discipline and support timing are both clean. This guide's strength is in maintaining high real uptime while preserving party stability in pattern-heavy endgame encounters.
