# Boss Wipe Mechanics: Why Stagger Gauge Break Matters

*Author: Nirr | Original: https://www.inven.co.kr/board/aion2/6444/361*

## Why Stagger Gauge Matters

Conquest dungeon bosses have a green **Stagger Gauge** under their HP bar. Depleting it puts the boss in stagger, opening a **burst window**. Move behind the boss and dump skills that only work on staggered targets.

In later Conquest dungeons, failing to break the gauge in time triggers a **wipe pattern**. The key is to stagger the boss at the right moment and prevent the wipe.

For example, **Verk (Complete Form)** in Krao Cave (Conquest) locks his stagger gauge until 50% HP, when it unlocks. If you don't deplete it quickly enough, the party wipes. Always bring high stagger-damage Stigma skills and save them for this window.

## Focus on Stagger Damage

Each class has skills with **Stagger Gauge damage** shown in the tooltip. Use those when the gauge is active.

Generally, Stigma skills remove far more of the gauge than Mastery skills (typically 20–50). Save high-stagger Stigmas for clutch moments — their cooldowns are long.

## Bosses with Wipe Patterns (Stagger Gauge)

Currently known dungeons requiring stagger breaks:
- **Conquest Urugugu Gorge** (1,600+)
- **Conquest Barklon's Sky Island** (1,600+)
- **Transcendence Shattered Arcanis** (+1,200)

Recommended to study before going:
1. [Expedition/Conquest] Krao Cave (1,000+): Complete Verk (final boss)
2. [Expedition/Conquest] Draupnir (1,000+): Transcended Bakarma (final boss)
3. [Transcendence] Deus Research Base (1,200+): Atiel (final boss)

## Class Stagger Skills

Every class has at least one Stigma that removes 50 stagger. Bring a 50-stagger skill for dungeons; consider adding a 20-stagger one as well.

**Chanter (호법성)** has only one Stigma with stagger damage ([滅化] Melt), so be sure to bring it.
